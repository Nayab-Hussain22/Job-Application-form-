Professional car diagnostics begin with opening the hood and performing a thorough physical inspection. During this step, the engine oil level, coolant reservoir, and drive belts are checked to identify any signs of leakage, damage, or wear and tear. Next, the vehicle is started, and the dashboard warning lights, such as the **Check Engine Light**, are observed while carefully listening to the engine for any unusual noises, including knocking or squealing sounds. Finally, the most professional and reliable method is to use an **OBD2 scanner**, which is connected to the vehicle's diagnostic port to read technical error codes. This provides an accurate and detailed report of both visible and hidden engine faults, allowing for precise diagnosis and effective repairs.
